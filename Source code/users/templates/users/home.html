Artifact

Artifacts are abnormal findings in data that do not originate from the normal physiological or pathological processes. They can distort the accuracy of diagnostic results and need to be carefully identified and excluded.

Learn More

Extrahls

Extrahls refer to extraneous or unwanted signals that can interfere with the interpretation of diagnostic data. Proper filtering and analysis techniques are necessary to manage and mitigate these disturbances.

Learn More

Extrastole

Extrastole denotes abnormal heartbeats occurring outside the normal rhythm. These irregularities can be symptomatic of underlying cardiovascular issues and require accurate monitoring for effective management.

Learn More

Murmur

A murmur is an abnormal sound heard during a heartbeat, often caused by turbulent blood flow within the heart. Murmurs can indicate various heart conditions and may necessitate further diagnostic evaluation.

Learn More

Normal

Normal refers to findings within the expected range of physiological or diagnostic parameters. Regular monitoring and maintaining normal levels are crucial for overall health and effective disease prevention.

Learn More

Unlabel

Unlabel refers to data or findings that have not been classified or identified within a diagnostic framework. Unlabeled data requires further analysis and interpretation to determine its significance and relevance.
